Banga

This wonderful recipe originates from the Itsekiris. This soup is made primarily from the palm fruit extract which contains valuable nutrients e.g helps reduce vitamin E deficiency. Also known as Ofe Akwu in the Niger-Delta, this flavorful dish is served with starchy staples like fufu, eba, or pounded yam. It represents a connection to the region's agricultural heritage and traditional cooking methods.

Maafe

Maafe, also known as groundnut stew is another flavorful cuisine that dominates major parts of West Africa particularly countries like Senegal and Mali. With groundnut as its major ingredient, and the trinity of tomatoes, onion and chillies amongst other spices. Its preparations require expertise and is traditionally served with white rice, potatoes or fufu. Its a great way to explore West African cuisine.

Egusi

Egusi soup is a rich cuisine that traditionally originates from West Africa. It is mainly made of grinded melon seeds and commonly includes vegetables, aromatic spices like cloves, nutmeg, and meat or fish. This traditional cuisine is easy to make yet splendid. It helps skin improvement as it contains antioxidant properties that help to fight aging and slows down the aging process.

Vegetables

West Africa has a wide varieties of veges which includes, Okro, this is used as a thickening agent for stews and soups and is a good source of fiber, vitamins (C, K), and antioxidants. Greens (Efo-tete), these are leafy veges rich in vitamins (A, C, K), iron, calcium, and antioxidants. Tomatoes (tomati) are a staple vegetable in West African cuisine, used in sauces, stews and rich in vitamins (C, K), antioxidants, and lycopene.

Salads

Though salads may not be prominent compared to stews and grain. Here are some nutritious salad creations that reflect the region's culinary diversity. Garden Egg Salad also known as aubergines or eggplants in some regions, Avocado salad is a versatile ingredient that features in many West African cuisines. Fruit Salad with Tamarind Dressing, this is made with a variety of tropical fruits like pineapple, mango, papaya, and citrus fruits, drizzled with a sweet-sour tamarind dressing.

Fruits

The West African region boasts a wide array of flavors, from tropical and exotic fruits to more common varieties that are integral to their cuisine. These includes, Pineapples are rich in vitamin C, manganese, bromelain, and fiber. Papayas also rich in vitamins C. Soursop is a spiky green fruit with a creamy white flesh and a tangy-sweet flavor reminiscent of a combination of strawberries and pineapple. Baobab fruit is known for its velvety citrus flavor and powdery texture and rich in vitamin C, fiber, antioxidants, and minerals like potassium and calcium.
